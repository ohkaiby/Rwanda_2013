# Some Notes from Rwanda - April 22-26
So far I've only served at the two Unity Conferences in Nyagatare and Kigali for the Rwandan pastors. There's too much for me to put into formal writing at the moment, so you get to read my shorthand notes. I intend to make a presentation (both live **and** online). Here's what I've learned so far.

## In General
* Rwandans are resilient
    * walking miles everywhere, cars are a luxury... while carrying heavy bushes, bananas, logs, tree trunks, etc. on their heads.
    * pastor Pauline going for a walk and being uncomplaining
* gangs don't exist
* theft rate is high
* Walking in Rwanda is like grade school all over again: people stare at you / take photos of you because they rarely see Chinese people.
* Traffic is crazy. Matatus are crazy. Lack of safety is crazy.
* Air is dirty.
* Showers... are unreliable.
* food = starch > veggies > meat.
* One saturday per month from 7AM to 12PM is for cleaning. This is to keep the streets / country clean. It started after the genocide in order to clean up the dead bodies.
* It's common to just throw trash on the ground (ppl clean it up later). It's an old way of doing things. Not allowed on the city streets though.
* The children aren't afraid of white / Chinese strangers. They'll run right up to you to hug you / get photos taken (if they see your camera).
* I had to take photos after I've already gotten enough just to make the conference attendees feel special.

## Churches
* They do so much with so little.
* Most pastors don't have a car, yet they plant churches miles apart from one another.
* Pastors don't make a salary at all. Live day by day, by faith.
* Them pastors... they're hungry. Fervently taking notes during messages.
* They know how to worship.
* The current translation of the bible in Rwanda isn't even that accurate because it was translated with a Catholic bias (need to confirm).
* There's a new bible being circulated that has more accurate translations. We bought enough for their pastor's fellowship (PFF = pastor family fellowship).
* Having a church building is a sign that God is on your side. Holding services in your home is looked down upon.
* People don't have that big of an issue with doubts when being evangelized to. A lot of people come to faith just from street evangelism.
* Prosperity gospel is an issue. There's a lack of teaching.
* A question was asked regarding the role of a mother-church's senior pastor in the raising of new leaders at a church plant that has a senior pastor there.
* Welcoming introductions are looooong (30m).

## Struggles of Rwandan Pastors

### Poverty
* physical - they don't get a salary. live day by day.
* spiritual - they feel inadequate in leading their flocks, lack of teaching. Kind of just thrown into the field with an expectation to “figure it out.”

### Rivalry / Lack of Unity
* can't really meet together w/ other pastors because of lack of trust. ulterior motives.
* people are still going to join Rwandan pastors with similar background.
* these things cause a divide / bitterness between one another. leads to hard-heartedness / lack of forgiveness.

### Forgiveness
* People flocking to other churches when you aren't doing so well (“If God was on your side you'd be doing better, I'm going to another church where God is.”) – Actually that's not all that different from people ditching churches here [church shopping mentality].
* People leaving after you've invested time / money in them.
